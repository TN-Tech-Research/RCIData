Printing defects were the most common issue and nearly all reported issues were resolved. Missed events due to lack of awareness point directly to communication gaps — calendar invites address both problems simultaneously. Scheduling conflicts are harder to resolve operationally; wider scheduling windows may help.

Looking ahead to RCIS 2027

Three priority areas emerging from participant feedback.

01
Strengthen communication logistics
  • Add calendar invites for all event times and locations
  • Clarify poster format requirements earlier with visual aids (trifold, dimensions)
  • Send explicit pre-event participant guidance
02
Reduce preventable print issues
  • Build a centralized print portal with postscript preview capability
  • Provide embedded-font and margin checklist at submission
  • Offer modernized templates on accessible platforms (Canva)
03
Build engagement and presence
  • Explore live or asynchronous verbal presentation competition category.
  • Increase networking and faculty feedback time or enhance the impact of interactions within the same timeframe;
  • Build on existing advisor relationships to solve load balancing issues for judges
  • Pursue industry partnerships for broader recognition
RCIS 2026 demonstrated strong faculty engagement, digital infrastructure, and participant enthusiasm. Addressing logistical pain points around communication and printing can drive significant gains with relatively low effort. Longer-term growth opportunities could include exploring additional event formats and building stronger industry connections.
